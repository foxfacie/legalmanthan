Maintenance and alimony are essential aspects of matrimonial law, ensuring financial stability for a spouse after separation or divorce. In India, these provisions differ across various personal laws but share the common objective of preventing financial hardship.  

---

## 📌 Hindu Marriage Act, 1955  

- **Section 24 (Interim Maintenance):**  
  Either spouse (husband or wife) may claim financial support during ongoing legal proceedings if they lack independent income. It also covers litigation expenses.  

- **Section 25 (Permanent Alimony and Maintenance):**  
  Courts can order one spouse to provide financial assistance to the other after divorce. The support may be a lump sum or periodic payments, based on factors like income, property, conduct, and circumstances of both parties.  

- **Applicability:**  
  Applies to Hindus, Buddhists, Jains, and Sikhs.  

---

## 📌 Special Marriage Act, 1954  

- **Section 36 (Interim Maintenance):**  
  The wife may claim maintenance during the pendency of legal proceedings.  

- **Section 37 (Permanent Alimony):**  
  Courts may grant long-term financial support post-divorce, considering both parties’ earning capacity and conduct.  

- **Applicability:**  
  Governs inter-faith and civil marriages. The provisions are largely similar to those under the Hindu Marriage Act.  

---

## 📌 Indian Divorce Act, 1869 (Christians)  

- **Section 36 (Interim Maintenance):**  
  The wife can seek temporary financial support during divorce proceedings.  

- **Section 37 (Permanent Alimony):**  
  Courts may order post-divorce financial assistance. The amount is determined after evaluating income, assets, and circumstances of both spouses.  

- **Applicability:**  
  Specifically governs Christians in India.  

---

## 📌 Parsi Marriage and Divorce Act, 1936  

- **Section 39 (Permanent Alimony):**  
  Provides financial assistance to the wife after divorce. Courts assess factors such as the standard of living maintained during the marriage and the financial condition of both parties.  

- **Applicability:**  
  Applies to Parsis (Zoroastrians) in India.  

---

## 📌 Muslim Personal Law  

- **Governing Laws:**  
  - Muslim Personal Law (Shariat) Application Act, 1937  
  - Muslim Women (Protection of Rights on Divorce) Act, 1986  

- **Maintenance During Marriage:**  
  A Muslim husband is bound to maintain his wife during the subsistence of marriage.  

- **Post-Divorce Maintenance:**  
  - A divorced woman is entitled to maintenance during the *iddat* (waiting) period.  
  - Beyond iddat, she is entitled to a “fair and reasonable provision and maintenance,” often through **mehr (dower)** or other mutually agreed settlement.  
  - In certain cases, if she cannot maintain herself, she may seek support under **Section 125 of the Code of Criminal Procedure (CrPC), 1973**, irrespective of religion.  

---

## 🔑 Key Takeaways  

- Maintenance aims to prevent economic hardship after separation or divorce.  
- Provisions exist across **Hindu, Muslim, Christian, and Parsi personal laws**, as well as under the **Special Marriage Act**.  
- Courts consider factors like income, assets, conduct, standard of living, and needs of the spouse before granting alimony.  
- **Section 125 CrPC** provides a secular remedy, ensuring maintenance for wives, children, and parents across all religions.
